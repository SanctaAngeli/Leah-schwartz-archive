# Nano Banana 2 — page mockup prompts (the "imagined" style)

Use these to visualise the rest of the site in the same premium, tactile,
museum-grade language as the single-painting front door and the studio-table
hero. One concept per page. Generate 3–4 variants each; we build from the
winner.

## House style (prepend to any prompt, or trust the per-prompt copy)

> A website homepage/section design mockup, desktop 16:9. Warm gallery-cream
> ground (#FAF6EC) with faint cotton-paper grain. Typography: a refined thin
> serif for titles (think EB Garamond), small letter-spaced caps for labels,
> occasional casual ink handwriting for her voice. Muted earth + watercolor
> palette — the art carries the colour. Enormous calm negative space,
> Apple-product-page restraint, no stock-photo gloss, no neon, no modern UI
> chrome. Reverent, intimate, the feeling of a quiet museum.

When a prompt says **(feed her work)**, attach real paintings from
`nano-banana-studio/` (or `public/artworks/full/`) as `@img1, @img2 …` and add:
*"the watercolours must be faithful reproductions of the attached works, not
reinterpretations."*

## 1 · The Gallery / Themes — "twelve lit rooms"
```
A website gallery page mockup, desktop 16:9, warm cream space. A long
museum wall seen slightly in perspective, hung with a measured row of
framed watercolours at eye level, each lit by a soft warm pool of light,
the floor a pale polished concrete reflecting faint colour. Generous air
above the frames. A thin serif chapter title floats top-left ("STILL LIFE
· in love with simple objects"), tiny letter-spaced caps. No buttons —
the paintings are the navigation. Calm, spacious, reverent, the hush of an
empty gallery before opening. (feed her work)
```

## 2 · Leah, In Her Own Words — "the manuscript"
```
A website reading-page mockup, desktop 16:9, cream paper ground. Centered,
a single tall column of elegant serif body text as if set by a fine press,
wide margins, a large drop-cap initial. In the right margin, a small
faithful watercolour and, lower, a vintage black-and-white snapshot, both
sitting on the paper like tipped-in plates with faint deckled edges and
soft shadow. A line of casual ink handwriting as a pulled quote. No UI.
Scholarly, intimate, like reading her private book. (feed her work + a
B&W photo)
```

## 3 · At Her Age / Eras — "a life laid out"
```
A website timeline page mockup, desktop 16:9, cream ground. A single
hair-fine horizontal rule spanning the full width at mid-height, with a
slim band of watercolour colour flowing along it that shifts from sober
1940s browns to 1970s Mediterranean blues to late blooming pinks. Above
the line, one vintage black-and-white portrait of the artist at this age,
small, deckled. Below, a short paragraph of fine serif prose and a quiet
year ("1985"). Vast calm space. Data-as-poetry, dignified. (feed a B&W
photo)
```

## 4 · Color Atlas — "her chromatic life"
```
A website data-art page mockup, desktop 16:9, cream ground. Hundreds of
slim vertical colour stripes spanning the full width like a woven spectrum
or a long horizon of pigment, sampled from a painter's life's work —
muted ochres bleeding into cobalt and turquoise into soft rose, each
stripe with watercolour granulation so it reads as paint not pixels. A
single thin serif title and a faint year axis beneath. Otherwise empty.
Stunning, scholarly, restrained, a museum infographic made beautiful.
```

## 5 · Obsessions — "the subjects she returned to"
```
A website page mockup, desktop 16:9, cream ground. A horizontal sequence
of the same subject painted again and again — one pear, then a row of
variations of that pear across years — arranged like a contact sheet or a
naturalist's specimen plate, each small image with a faint deckled edge
and a tiny pencilled date. Thin serif heading "ONE PEAR · NINE TIMES",
small caps. Lots of paper around them. Quiet, obsessive, tender. (feed her
work — several studies of one subject)
```

## 6 · Places / The World Map — "where the paint was made"
```
A website map page mockup, desktop 16:9, warm cream ground. A faint,
hand-drawn-feeling world map in pale sepia line, almost a watermark, no
satellite gloss. A scattering of small deckled watercolour vignettes
pinned at their locations (a Greek hill town on the Aegean, a green
California hill, a Kyoto canal), each tethered to its point by a hair-fine
line and a tiny serif place name. Travel-notebook intimacy, cartography as
memory, not a tech map. (feed travel works)
```

## 7 · Constellation — "the mind of the archive"
```
A website page mockup, desktop 16:9, deep soft warm-grey fading to cream
at the edges, faint paper texture. A slow constellation of small circular
nodes joined by hair-fine glowing threads — some nodes tiny faithful
watercolour thumbnails, others fine-serif word-nodes ("Mt. Tam", "Herman",
"Naxos", "Irises"); node size varies by importance, threads brighten where
ideas connect. Dreamlike, intelligent, watercolour-soft not sci-fi. (feed
a few small works)
```

## 8 · The Last Paintings — "the quiet room"
```
A website page mockup, desktop 16:9. A near-empty room in deep, calm
warm light, a single late watercolour hung large and alone on a far wall,
a wooden bench facing it, long shadows, dust in a shaft of window light.
Far more emptiness than image. A few words of her late writing set very
small in fine serif in the lower corner. Grave, hushed, valedictory — the
gravity of an ending. (feed a late work)
```

## 9 · Artwork close-reading — "her voice beside the work"
```
A website artwork detail mockup, desktop 16:9, cream ground. Left two
thirds: one watercolour shown large on a deckled paper ground with a soft
true shadow, as if pinned to a gallery wall. Right third: a narrow column
— a fine serif title and provenance line (year · medium · dimensions),
then a short passage of her own words in casual ink handwriting about this
exact painting. Nothing else. Reverent, scholarly, the painting and her
voice alone together. (feed one work)
```

## 10 · About / Estate — "who she was"
```
A website about-page mockup, desktop 16:9, cream ground. A single large
vintage black-and-white photograph of the artist in her studio, deckled
and tipped onto the paper with soft shadow, set left of centre. To the
right, a tall column of fine serif biography prose with a handwritten
signature beneath. Wide quiet margins, one small caption. The dignity of
a monograph's opening spread, not a corporate "about us". (feed a B&W
studio photo of Leah)
```

### Notes
- Keep the **cream ground + deckled edge + soft true shadow** through every
  page — it is now the site's signature; consistency is what makes it feel
  like one museum.
- For pages that are inherently interactive (Constellation, Atlas, Canvas),
  the mock only needs to capture the *resting state* — we build the motion.
- Once you pick winners, drop them in `public/textures/` or a `mocks/`
  folder and I'll build the page to match, the way we did the front door
  and the studio table.
