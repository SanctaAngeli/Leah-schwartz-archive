## OLD STUFF
*salvage of thirty years*

These paintings were done during the unsettled years of my life. Until
I was almost forty, I didn't have the kind of stability or free time necessary
for producing a consistent body of work. Most of the paintings I did in
my teens and twenties (portraits of friends, street scenes, farm houses, still
lifes, landscapes) are lost or disintegrated. I had no money for art materials
and painted on whatever surface was available: cardboard, sheetrock,
wood or paper.
When I was sixteen I received an anonymous gift, a complete set of oil
paints, brushes, linseed oil, turpentine and palette in a beautiful wooden
box (I still have the box). I later found out that it had been sent by Gene
Brown, a friend who encouraged me to paint. I was overwhelmed. In all
my sixteen years I had never received a gift of such splendor (or any
splendor).
Up until then I had painted in watercolor, gouache and tempera. Ben
Shahn gave me some powdered pigments and showed me how to prepare
an egg yolk emulsion for egg tempera painting. The yolk was tossed from
hand to hand until the surface was dry, and then was picked up between
thumb and forefinger, punctured with a pin so the yolk could flow out
free of any membrane, and was then mixed with enough water to make a
nice emulsion. He also gave me a panel of beautiful paper glued to
masonite that I was afraid to "ruin." It was dauntingly elegant. When he
found out that I hadn't painted anything on it, he guessed why and took a
brush loaded with watercolor, smeared it over the board and said, "There,
now go ahead and paint something!" I don't know what has happened to
the painting of a group of children I painted on it.
