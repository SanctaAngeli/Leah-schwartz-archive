## SOCIAL COMMENT
*rising from yeasty times*

In the sixties and seventies, when the U.S was in a ferment of civil
rights demonstrations and Vietnam war protests, I expressed my concern
in painting, multimedia constructions, and collages which were seen by
thousands of people at the huge Sausalito Arts Festival. The festival was
re-created yearly by a particularly creative and lively group of painters,
sculptors, architects, musicians, potters and film makers. We all worked
for months at the construction and organization of this event which was
as much fun as the final three-day carnival.
The money from gate receipts was used to create THE SAUSALITO
ARTS CENTER in an unused elementary school building. We had an
auditorium for performing arts, gallery space, and well-equipped studios
and workshops which stimulated a wild surge of creativity until ...the
Sausalito School Board voted to tear the place down and " ...put up a
parking lot." There was barely time to remove the expensive equipment
before the bulldozers came and smashed the building to rubble. It was
hard to believe that long-hair phobia could cause such a disaster.
