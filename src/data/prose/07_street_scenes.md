## STREET SCENES
*the random moment*

When my husband used to make house-calls, I would occasionally go
with him to photograph the streets of San Francisco. He knows his way
around the neighborhoods of San Francisco and doesn't mind driving
slowly so I can snag a "good one." I like to be "invisible" when taking
pictures for reference material. When people know they are being
photographed they get a peculiar self-conscious look, even if they pretend
not to notice the camera, and I try to get that unselfconscious look that
people have when they feel anonymous. I love these glimpses of people on
the street, walking their dogs, waiting for buses, standing on street corners
waiting for the light to change. I'm also interested in "street furniture":
bus shelters, mailboxes, poles, posts, mysterious black boxes, hydrants,
curbs, manhole covers - things we all see but rarely notice.
Getting the film back from the processor is a treat, as there is so much
previously unnoticed stuff. I'll take a figure from one, a bit of debris or a
car from another, and sometimes I go out with a Polaroid to get
additional detail. When I used to take black and white photos and process
them myself it was a lot faster but nerve-wracking, as I never really
believed that acetate, light, and chemicals could capture a fleeting moment.
